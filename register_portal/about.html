About Our Project

The Living Lab Initiative represents a groundbreaking fusion of environmental science, blockchain technology, and anthroposophical education. Located at Freie Waldorfschule Frankfurt (Oder) in Brandenburg, Germany, we have created one of the world's first educational environmental monitoring systems that rewards students and stewards with blockchain-based reciprocity credits for their contributions to citizen science.

🌍 Our Mission

To transform school gardens into "Living Laboratories" where students learn environmental science, data literacy, and blockchain technology through direct observation and stewardship of nature, creating a reciprocal economy that values both ecological data and educational engagement.

What Makes Us Unique

We are pioneering a new model for environmental education that bridges traditional Waldorf pedagogy with cutting-edge Web3 technology:

  • First Waldorf school with blockchain environmental monitoring
  • First educational reciprocal token economy for citizen science
  • First phenomenological observation system for IoT sensors
  • First integration of Steiner pedagogy with Web3 technology
  • First school-based reciprocal tokenization system rewarding environmental data

Our Living System

🔬 Environmental Monitoring

Our senseBox MCU environmental station continuously monitors seven key parameters in the school garden, transmitting data every 15 minutes:

  • Air temperature and humidity (HDC1080 sensor)
  • Soil moisture and temperature (SMT50 sensor)
  • Atmospheric pressure (BMP280 sensor)
  • Light intensity for photosynthesis (LTR329 sensor)
  • UV radiation levels (VEML6070 sensor)
  • Advanced calculations: VPD, ET0, heat index, dew point
  • Astronomical data: sunrise/sunset, moon phase, photoperiod

🪙 UBEC Reciprocity Credits (UBECrc)

UBEC stands for Universal Basic Environmental Contribution. These are utility tokens on the Stellar blockchain that recognize and reward the value provided by environmental data contributors and infrastructure stewards.

How It Works:

  • Students and stewards maintain environmental monitoring equipment
  • Each valid observation generates 7.14 UBECrc tokens
  • Tokens represent reciprocal exchange for infrastructure and data provision
  • System operates on principles of fair recognition and balanced reciprocity
  • Approximately 714 UBECrc generated per device daily

📊 Data Architecture

Our system implements a sophisticated dual-schema database design separating hardware sensor data from phenomenological observations, with blockchain verification and IPFS permanent storage ensuring data integrity and immutability.

Anthroposophical Approach

Following Rudolf Steiner's insights, our project recognizes four essential realms in living systems:

🌍 The Physical

Measurable matter - soil moisture, temperature, tangible data

💧 The Etheric

Life forces - humidity flow, atmospheric pressure, circulation of energy

🌬️ The Astral

Sensitivity and response - light for photosynthesis, UV radiation, plant awareness

🔥 The Ego/Spirit

Organizing principle - warmth as carrier of cosmic will, growth direction

Our sensors don't merely collect data—they observe the interplay of these four realms in real-time, helping students develop "living thinking" that grasps wholes rather than parts, processes rather than products, relationships rather than isolated facts.

Educational Impact

The Living Lab engages students across multiple learning domains:

  • Environmental Science: Direct observation of climate and soil systems
  • Data Science: Collection, analysis, and interpretation of real-world data
  • Blockchain Technology: Understanding distributed ledgers and tokenomics
  • IoT & Electronics: Hands-on experience with sensors and connectivity
  • Reciprocal Economics: Value exchange, fair recognition, sustainable systems
  • Phenomenology: Goethean observation and holistic understanding

Our comprehensive curriculum materials are available in both German and English, designed to integrate seamlessly with Waldorf pedagogy while meeting STEM education standards.
